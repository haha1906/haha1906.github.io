"The Thief" is Caughta
When the time came for the brothers to go home, Hazrat Yūsuf instructed his palace manager saying: "Fill their sacks with grain to the maximum. Then put everybody's money in the mouth of their sacks. Also put my silver cup into the youngest one's sack." When morning came, all of them took their donkeys and started on their journey. When they were still not too far off, Yūsuf (a.s.) said to his palace manager: "Now quickly go after them. When you catch them, say: Why did you repay kindness with evil? Why did you steal my master's cup? The palace manager quickly went after them. When he caught up with them, he repeated the words his master had told him to say. They replied, "What are you saying? We would never do such a thing! We have even brought you the money we found in our sacks last time. So why would we steal something from the house of your master? Go ahead and check. If any of us have the thing you mentioned, you have the right to kill him, and the rest of us will be slaves for your master." "Very good. If the cup is found, I will enslave him and keep him. The rest of you can go on your way," said the palace manager.
All of them quickly put their sacks down and opened them up. The palace manager started searching everybody's sack, starting from the oldest brother. The cup, of course, was in Binyāmin's sack. The brothers were terribly upset and cried out in sorrow. Then they loaded their sacks on their donkeys, and returned to the city with the palace manager.

Yahūdā (a.s.) offers Himself They said, "Mighty governor, he has an elderly father. Take one of us in his place. We see that you are a very good man." (Yūsuf, 12:78).
When they came back to the city, Yūsuf (a.s.) was still in his palace. The brothers came and fell to the ground before Hazrat Yūsuf. "What is this you have done?" said Yūsuf. "Did you not think that a powerful man like me is able to reveal secrets?" Yahūdā replied, "Oh my lord! We do not know even how to answer. How do we defend ourselves? Allāh is punishing us for the sin we have done. Now all of us will be your slaves, including our brother from whom the silver cup was found." "No," said Hazrat Yūsuf. "I will enslave only the man who stole the cup. The others will go home."
Then Yahūdā stepped forward and said, "My lord, I know you have power like Firʿawn does. Please do not be angry, and let me say something. When you asked us: 'Is your father alive, and do you have other brothers?', we said, 'We have an old father. We were twelve brothers. The youngest is our father's son, who was born in his old age and is at home now. His brother died and now he is the only brother we have from that wife of our father. This is why our father loves him very much.' Then you said, 'Go and bring that brother to me, I want to see him with my eyes.' We said, 'If we separate him from his father, his father will die.' But you said, 'If you do not bring your youngest brother, do not show up.' When we went to our father, we told him what you said. Then later on our father said, 'Go back again and buy more grain.' We said, 'No, if you do not give permission for your youngest son to go, we cannot go to Miṣr. The governor of Miṣr said that if we do not bring our youngest brother, we should not even show up.' Then our father said to us, 'You know, Rāḥīl bore me two sons. I lost one of them. He went and did not return. He became food for wild animals, obviously, and I never saw him again. Now if you take this one as well, and if something happens to him, you will send me to the grave alive." My lord, if we go now to our father, and if his youngest son is not with us, my father will die from a broken heart. His life is hanging on this boy. I guaranteed my father that I would bring our youngest brother safe and sound. I told him, 'If I don't bring him back to you, I will bear the blame forever.' So my lord, I beg you, please, make me a slave instead of my younger brother. Let the boy return to his father. How can I go to my father without my younger brother? How can I watch this disaster happen to my father?!"
