In the presence of Firʿawn "We sent Mūsā to Firʿawn and his leading supporters with Our signs, but they rejected them. See the fate of those who used to spread corruption." (Al-Aʿrāf 7:103)
Then Mūsā and Hārūn (a.s.), together with the elders of Banī Isrāʾīl, went to Firʿawn and said: "Your Majesty, Allāh (s.w.t.) commanded you saying, 'Let My people go to the desert and worship Me.'" Firʿawn replied, "Who is your Allāh (s.w.t.), that by his word I should let Banī Isrāʾīl go? I do not know Allāh".
 
(At that time the people of Miṣr were idolaters and worshipped the ṭāghūt a. That is why nabī Mūsā later on had to explain who Allāh was by calling Him "Our Allāh".)
 
"Your Majesty, our Ilāh b has revealed His desire to us. Give us permission, and let us go a journey of three days so we can offer Him sacrifices, otherwise He may send a sword or a plague on us." Firʿawn replied, "Mūsā and Hārūn, why are you distracting so many people from their work? Now everybody, go away, go to work!" Then he ordered his overseers and headworkers by saying, "From now on do not give straw to the ʿIbrāniyyūn for making bricks. They must find the necessary straw themselves. But the number of bricks must not be less than before. It seems that their work is not very hard! Otherwise they would not beg me to let them go and offer sacrifices. Make their work so hard they won't have time to listen to such nonsense."
The oppression gets stronger
Then the overseers and the headworkers announced to the Banī Isrāʾīl: "According to the order of Firʿawn, from now on you won't be given straw to make bricks. Find it wherever you can. But the daily number of bricks must not be less than before". So Banī Isrāʾīl spread out all over Miṣr searching for straw. And the Miṣriy overseers were oppressing them saying, "Do not lessen the number of bricks!" They were beating the ʿIbrāniy headworkers, saying: "Why aren't you doing your duty like before?!" Finally, the ʿIbrāniy headworkers came to Firʿawn and complained, "Why are they tormenting us so much? They won't give us straw but they are demanding the same number of bricks as before. They are beating us for no reason. It is not our fault." "You are just lazy!" said Firʿawn. "Otherwise you would not say 'Let us go and offer sacrifices to our Allāh'. Go, and do your work! You won't be given even one bundle of straw!" Hearing this the headworkers felt that they got in a very bad situation. As they were coming out the royal palace, they saw Hārūn and Mūsā (a.s.) who were waiting outside the palace, and said to them: "May Allāh (s.w.t.) punish you! You put Firʿawn and people of the palace against us! You have put a sword in their hands — an excuse to kill us".c
The promise of Allāh (s.w.t.)
Then Hazrat Mūsā went and prayed to Allāh (s.w.t.) saying: "My Rabb! Why did you let evil come to this people? Did you bring me here for this purpose? Ever since I delivered Your word to Firʿawn, he has been treating this nation cruelly. But You have not rescued Your people!"
