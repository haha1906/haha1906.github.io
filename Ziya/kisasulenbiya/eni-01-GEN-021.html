The birth of Hazrat Isḥāq
Allāh (s.w.t.) fulfilled His promise: Mother Sārah became pregnant, and bore a son to Ibrāhīm (a.s.) at the time which had been said. Prophet Ibrāhīm named his son Isḥāq and on the 8th day he circumcised him, as Allāh had commanded. At that time Ibrāhīm (a.s.) was 100 yearsa old. Sārah said, "Allāh has given me laughter. Now whoever hears it will laugh with me.b Who thought that I would nurse a baby one day?! Even though my husband has become old, I have borne him a son!"
 
The Story of Ismāʿīl (a.s.) “Mention too, in the Qur an, the story of Ismāʿīl. He was true to his promise, a messenger and a prophet.” (Maryam 19:54).
\it Our mother Sārah was jealous of Hājar. Many scholars narrated about thisc. As Ṭabarī writes, Sārah drove Hājar twice from the house.d
 
One day Ibrāhīm (a.s.) gave a big feast on the occasion of his son Isḥāq being weaned (separated from mother's milk). When Sārah saw that Hajar's son Ismāʿīl was sitting and laughing, she went to Ibrāhīm (a.s.) and demanded: “Drive away Hājar with her son! The son of a servant will not be a partner to the inheritance my son will receive”. Hazrat Ibrāhīm got very upset because of the words of his wife. Because Ismāʿīl also was his son! But Allāh (s.w.t.) sent a wahy to Ibrāhīm (a.s.): “Do what Sārah said. My promise given about your descendant will be continued through Isḥāq. And don’t worry about Ismāʿīl and Hājar. I will keep them safe. I will make nations from Ismāʿīl’s offspring. Because he is also your child.”
 
\it Yes, this also was a test that came from Allāh.e Is it easy to separate from your child? Ismāʿīl (a.s.) was the eldest son of Nabī Ibrāhīm.
The prayer of Ibrāhīm (a.s.)
\it In Qurʾān al-Karīm, there is a prayer of Ibrāhīm (a.s.): “Our Lord, I have established some of my offspring in an uncultivated valley, close to Your Sacred House, Lord, so that they may keep up the prayer. Make people’s hearts turn to them, and provide them with produce, so that they may be thankful.” f
\it After Hājar and Ismāʿīl (a.s.) moved to a new location, Allāh (s.w.t.) Himself cared for them. He himself kept them under His protection. The appearance of a well in the desert is also a dalīl to Allāh's mercy.
A spring appears in the desert
\it According to a ḥadīth of our prophet (s.a.v.) Hājar laid down Ismāʿīl (a.s.) under the shade of a bush \it and hung the waterskin on a branch. She, as well as her son were drinking water from that waterskin. After a while the water was all gone. g When water was gone, Hājar said: “It is better not to see the death of my son”, -- and h went on a distance.
 
Then she climbed up a hill and looked around seeking for water. Hoping to hear someone’s voice, she looked around. But there was no sound, no one was in sight. Then she ran down, looked around longingly, but still could see no one. Then she just wept and wandered between two hills.i
 
Then Allāh’s angel appeared and said: “Hājar, hey Hājar, why are you crying? Don’t be afraid, your (two of you) cries reached the sky. Come on, get up and get your son up! Allāh will create a big nation from his offspring”. Allāh (s.w.t.) had opened a well for them there, but Hājar hadn’t seen it yet. Then Allāh (s.w.t.) directed her sight to the well. She looked – water! The water that gives life! She hastened and drank water. Then she filled the waterskin and gave water to the son. So, Allāh (s.w.t.) was always with them. Ismāʿīl grew in desert and became a skilful archer. Later his mother got a girl from Miṣr for him.
