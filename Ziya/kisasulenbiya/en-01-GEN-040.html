THE BAKER AND BUTLER SEE DREAMSa
A long time passed after that. One day Firʿawn's baker and butler offended their royal master and both were put in the prison where Yūsuf (a.s.) was. The commander of the Palace guards appointed Yūsuf (a.s.) to serve them. After that, quite some time passed, and one day at night both the butler and the baker saw dreams. Each of their dreams had its own meaning. In the morning Yūsuf (a.s.) came to them and saw that both of them looked troubled. "What happened?" he asked. "Why do you look upset today?" They replied, "Yesterday we both happened to see dreams. But there is nobody here who can interpret our dreams," they said. Then Yūsuf (a.s.) said to them: "The ability to interprete dreams is given by Allāh. Tell me, what dream have you seen? Allāh (s.w.t.) may help with the interpretation." First the butler narrated the dream he saw: "I saw a grapevine in front of me. It had three branches. Suddenly the branches budded, blossomed, and clusters of grapes ripened from the flowers. Then I looked and saw the wine cup of Firʿawn in my hand. I picked the grapes and squeezed the juice into the cup. Then I gave the cup to Firʿawn."
Allāh interprets the dreams
Yūsuf (a.s.) said: "Here is the interpretation of your dream: the three branches mean three days. In three days Firʿawn will free you from prison and will appoint you again as his butler. When your life gets back to normal, please, remember me also. Speak to Firʿawn about me and ask him to free me from this prison. I was taken from my homeland by force. I was put in prison here even though I did not any commit any crime here in Miṣr either."b
The baker saw that the butler's dream had a good interpretation, so he also told his dream to Yūsuf: "I saw in my dream that there were three baskets on my head. In the top basket there were different kinds of baked food for Firʿawn. And a flock of birds was eating that baked food in the basket." "The interpretation of your dream is like this," said Yūsuf (a.s.). "The three baskets are three days. In three days, Firʿawn will will hang your body on a pole. Then birds will come and peck at it."
The dreams come true "Fellow prisoners, one of you will serve his master with wine; the other will be hung and the birds will peck at his head. That is the end of the matter on which you asked my opinion." (Yūsuf 12:41) c "Yūsuf said to the one he knew would be saved, 'Mention me to your master,' but Shayṭān made him forget to do this".d
Firʿawn's birthday came three days later. On that occasion, he had a big feast prepared for all of his officials. Then, exactly as Hazrat Yūsuf interpreted, the butler was appointed to his previous position, and the baker was hung on a pole. After getting restored to his position, however, the butler forgot about Yūsuf (a.s.).
