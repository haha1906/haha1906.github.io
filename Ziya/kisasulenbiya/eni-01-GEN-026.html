Allāh (s.w.t.) reminds Isḥāq (a.s.) of His covenant promises
A famine started in the land like the famine that happened before in the time of Ibrāhīm (a.s.). For this reason Hazrat Isḥāq went to Jarār, to the land of the Qawm al-Jālūt, where Abū Malik was king. And then Allāh (s.w.t.) appeared to Isḥāq (a.s.) and said: "Do not go to Miṣr. Do not leave the territory of Kanʿān. I will be with you, and I will bless you. I will give all these lands to you and to your children. I will fulfill the promise which I swore to your father Ibrāhīm. Ibrāhīm listened to me. He obeyed My commandments, laws and rules. This is why I will multiply your descendants like stars in the sky. I will give all these lands to you (plural). All the nations of the earth will find blessing through your zurriyya."
Promises given through the Covenant
So, Allāh (s.w.t.) promised Isḥāq (a.s.) three things:
  that he will have many descendants,
  that his descendants will possess the earth
  and through his descendant all the nations of the earth will find blessing.
If you remember, the prophet Ibrāhīm was also promised these same things.
Isḥāq (a.s.) and the king of Jarār
So, Hazrat Isḥāq was living in Jarār. He was afraid of people, thinking, if I say that she is my wife, they will kill me in order to marry her. So, he was telling everybody that Ribqah was his sister. (Ribqah was indeed very beautiful.) Quite some time passed in between. One day the king of the Qawm al-Jālūt looked out a window and saw that Hazrat Isḥāq was playing with Ribqah. Then he called Isḥāq (a.s.) to his palace and said: "It looks like she is actually your wife! Why did you say she is your sister?" Hazrat Isḥāq said: "I was afraid to call her my wife, because I thought I would be killed." The king said: "How could you do this? Did not you think that someone could marry her and join with her? You almost made us commit sin!" Then he announced to everybody saying: "Whoever touches this man or his wife, will be killed!"
The blessing of Allāh (s.w.t.)
That year Hazrat Isḥāq's crops gave 100 fold harvest! Allāh (s.w.t.) had blessed him very much. His wealth increased more and more, and he became a very rich man. He owned flocks of sheep and herds of cattle and slaves. Then the Qawm al-Jālūt started to envy him and filled up his wells with dirt. Ibrāhīm (a.s.) himself had these wells dug when he was alive. Then the king of Jarār called Hazrat Isḥāq to himself and said: "You became quite rich and powerful. Now you have to move out of our land." So Hazrat Isḥāq moved to the Valley of Jarār and reopened the wells which were initially dug during the life of his father Ibrāhīm (a.s.), and later on were filled by the Qawm al-Jālūt.
ʿIsāw's wives
When ʿIsāw was 40 years old, he married Yihūdit daughter of Bīʾirī al-Ḥittiy a, then he married Bāsimah daughter of Īlūn al-Ḥitt. Hazrat Isḥāq and Ribqah were very very sad because of his pagan wives.
