Qāsim had a son named Riʿū, Riʿū had a son named Sirūgh, and Sirūgh had a son named Nāḥūr. Nāḥūr's son was Tīraḥa, who later became the father of Ibrāhīm (a.s.).
The story of Ibrāhīm (a.s.) "Allāh speaks the truth, so follow Ibrāhīm’s religion: he had true faith and he was never a mushrik (idolater)." (Āl ʾImrān 3:95)
The father of Ibrāhīm
Tīraḥ had three sons: Ibrāhīm \f + Lit. "Abrām"., Nāḥūr and Hārān. At that time they lived in the town of the al-Kasdāniyyūn, called Atūn. Then Hārān married, and had a son named Lūṭ, and daughters named Milkah and Yiskah. Later on he died in that land — in the town of Atūn, when his father was still alive. Ibrāhīm also got married, but his wife Sarāh was not able to get pregnant. One day Tīraḥ took his children and went in the direction of the land of Kanʿān. Since there was a desert in between, they had to go first to the town of Hārān in the north before going south to Kanʿān. But when they arrived in Hārān, they decided to stay there.
The childhood of Ibrāhīm (a.s.)
\it As it is written in many books, Ibrāhīm's (a.s.) people were worshipping lifeless idols, and would not acknowledge living Allāh. But Ibrāhīm (a.s.) was looking for answers for questions like: "Is there a Rabb over the world? Who is the rabb of all worlds?"
Searching for Allāh
"When the night grew dark over him he saw a star and said, ‘This is my Lord,’ but when it set, he said, ‘I do not like things that set.’ And when he saw the moon rising he said, ‘This is my Lord,’ but when it too set, he said, ‘If my Lord does not guide me, I shall be one of those who go astray.’ Then he saw the sun rising and cried, ‘This is my Lord! This is greater.’ But when the sun set, \it (he started thinking that there must be a greater Being who must have created all these. Then he said: ‘My people, I disown all that you worship beside God. I have turned my face as a true believer towards Him who created the heavens and the earth. I am not one of the mushriks.’" \it (Al-Anʿām, 6:76-79).
 
\it In one of those days Allāh (s.w.t.) gave him wahiy and said: "Aslim". \it The meaning is, "Submit to Me". Ibrāhīm immediately said, "Aslamtu li-rabbi l-ʿālamīn", b\it which means, "I've submitted, I believe in the Lord of the worlds". After that he tried to turn his father and his tribe away from worshipping idols and return to one Allāh, the owner of the earth and the sky.
Destroying the Idols
(Ibrāhīm) asked his father and his people, ‘What do you worship?’ They said, ‘We worship idols, and are constantly in attendance on them.’ He asked, ‘Do they hear you when you call? Do they help or harm you?’ They replied, ‘No, but this is what we saw our fathers doing.’.*
 
\it One day there was a festival. While everybody went to the festival, Ibrāhīm (a.s.) carried an ax and went to the idol-house. When he saw food in front of the idols, he said: "Do you not eat? Why do you not speak?" Then he turned and struck them with his right arm.c
 
\it In Sūra Anbiyāʾ in the Qur'an it is written: "He broke them all into pieces, but left the biggest one".d \it Then he hung the axe on the neck of that big idol and left the idol-house.
 
\it After a while the people returned from the festival and saw the idols broken and got angry, and asked Ibrāhīm (a.s.): "Was it you, Ibrāhīm, who did this to our gods?" \it He answered making fun of them, "No, it was done by the biggest of them– this one. Ask them, if they can talk." * \it They said, "You know that idols cannot speak!" Ibrāhīm (a.s.) said: "In that case, How can you worship what can neither benefit nor harm you, instead of Allah?"
Ibrāhīm (a.s.) Thrown into the Fire
\it After that the king of those peoplee called Ibrāhīm to his presence and said, "Who is your god? Have you ever seen him?" Ibrāhīm (a.s.) said, "My Lord is the one who resurrects and causes death". The king said, "I'm also able to do that. If two people are judged to be killed, I will kill one of them and the other one I forgive." Then Ibrāhīm (a.s.) said, "Allāh brings the sun from the east. (If you're powerful,) you bring it from the west!" * \it The king couldn't answer and was silenced.
 
\it After that, the king decided to kill Ibrāhīm (a.s.) in the fire. * \it When everything was ready, a big fire was lit, and they threw Ibrāhīm (a.s.) into the fire.
The Wisdom of the Story
\it Ibrāhīm (a.s.) put his tawakkul in Allāh even though they said that they would burn him in the fire. Allāh brought Ibrāhīm (a.s.) from the maqām of īmān to the maqām\wa of \wa tawakkul (or trust). In this kind of situation, every believer hopes that Allāh will rescue him. Because if Allāh wills, He will save us even from disasters like fire and water. And even if He doesn't save us, our trust in Allāh's compassion toward us should remain firm.
 
\it When Ibrāhīm (a.s.) was thrown into fire, Allāh (s.w.t.) saved him. In Qurʾān al-Karīm there is a command, "Fire, be cool and safe for Ibrāhīm!" * \it And even though the prophet Ibrāhīm's tribe planned to harm him, Allāh made them suffer the greatest loss.
