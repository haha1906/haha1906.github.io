The story of Hājar
The wife of Hazrat Ibrāhīm, Sārah, was not able to have children. For this reason she spoke to her husband about her Miṣriyya slave and said: "Here, Allāh has not given me children. Now you have relations with Hājar, maybe I will have a child from her." Hazrat Ibrāhīm agreed. This way, in 10 yearsa after moving to Kanʿān, Sārah gave her Miṣriyya slave Hājar to her husband as a wife. In no time Hājar became pregnant. And when she knew she was pregnant, she started looking down at her sister wife, Sārah. Then Sārah came to Ibrāhīm (a.s.) and complained: "It is all your fault! I put this slave into your arms. Now she became pregnant and stopped respecting me. May Allāh Himself judge between us!" Hazrat Ibrāhīm answered: "She is your own property! Do whatever your heart decides!" After this, Sārah started oppressing Hājar. Finally Hājar fledb from the house.
Allāh's angel turns Hājar back from her path
In the desert, when Hājar arrived at a spring in the road of Shūr, an angel of Allāh (s.w.t.) appeared to Hājar and asked: "You, Sārah's slave Hājar! Where are coming from and where are you going to?" Hājar answered: "I am running away from my (female) owner, Sārah." The angel told her: "No, return to your (female) owner. Do what she says! Allāh (s.w.t.) will support you. He will increase your descendants so much that nobody can count them. Here, you are pregnant, you will have a son. And you will name your son "Ismāʿīl", because Allāh heard your cries.c Your son will be an independent person like the field horse and a freedom-lover like the eagle. He will live in defiance of his brothers."
The birth of Ismāʿīl (a.s.)
So Hājar returned to Sārah. When the time came, she bore Hazrat Ibrāhīm a son. The prophet Ibrāhīm named his son Ismāʿīl. (At that time, Hazrat Ibrāhīm was 86 years old.d)
