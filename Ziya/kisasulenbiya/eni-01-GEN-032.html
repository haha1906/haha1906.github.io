Yaʿqūb (a.s.) sends a message to his brother
Yaʿqūb (a.s.) continued on his journey.a At that time ʿIsāw lived in the land of Idūm, in the place called Sharāhah. Hazrat Yaʿqūb sent messengers to his brother and instructed them carefully: "Go to my brother and say: 'Your slave Yaʿqūb sent us. Until now he has been living in the house of your uncle Labān. Now he owns much cattle and many donkeys, sheep, goats and male and female slaves. Now he is returning to his father's land. He sent us to inform you with the hope that you will be kind to him.'" The messengers went and returned and said: "We have met your brother. Now he is coming here with 400 people." Yaʿqūb (a.s.) became afraid. He divided his flocks and the people in his family into two groups. He was thinking to himself, 'If my brother attacks me, maybe at least one group can escape. Then he knelt and prayed: "Oh Allāh! You are the Ilāh of my grandfather Ibrāhīm and my father Isḥāq! You told me to return to my land, to the house of my father. And You said You would give me a blessing! I am not worthy of all the faithfulness and unfailing love you have shown me. When I left home, I did not have anything except the walking stick in my hand. Now I have many people and many flocks. I beg you, save me from my brother. I am afraid that he will attack and kill me, and my wives and children. You had promised me to bless me and to increase my descendants like the sands of the sea!"
Gifts sent to ʿIsāw
That evening, Yaʿqūb (a.s.) chose some animals to give as gifts to his brother ʿIsāw: 200 female goats, 20 male goats, 200 sheep, 20 rams, 30 camels with their young, 40 cows, 10 bulls, 20 female donkeys and 10 male donkeys. Then he put each of them into a separate flock, and appointed a servant for each and said: "Go ahead of me with the animals. Keep some distance between the herds." To the first servant he said: "When you meet my brother ʿIsāw, he will ask: 'Whose servants are you? Whose animals are these?' Then you should say: 'These belong to your brother Yaʿqūb. He has sent them to my master ʿIsāw as a gift. He is coming also behind us.'" And Yaʿqūb (a.s.) told his other servants who would lead the other flocks, "When you meet my brother ʿIsāw, say the same thing. Then do not forget to say: 'Your brother Yaʿqūb is coming behind us.'" He hoped that by sending these gifts ahead of himself, he win over his brother's heart and his brother would treat him well when they met.
An encounter at night
So Hazrat Yaʿqūb sent the flocks which he had set aside as gifts. Several hours later, at night, he took his two wives, two concubines, eleven sons and all his wealth to the other side of the river Yabbūq. Then he himself returned to his tent alone. Then an angel of Allāh came in the form of a man and wrestled with him until daybreak. Finally, seeing that he could not conquer Hazrat Yaʿqūb, he struck him heavily and dislocated his joint of his hip. Then he said: "Now let me go, the morning is almost here." "No,' Yaʿqūb (a.s.) said, 'I won't let you go unless you bless me." Then he asked: "What is your name?" "Yaʿqūb". "From now on, your name won't be Yaʿqūb. You will be called Isrāʾīl,b because you struggled with people all of your life and even with angel and withstood the trials of Allāh." "What is your name?" asked Yaʿqūb (a.s.). But he did not answer, saying: "Why do you ask?" Then he blessed Hazrat Yaʿqūb. (Later on Hazrat Yaʿqūb named that place Faniʾīl", saying "I have been with the angel of Allāh face to face, but I am still alive." When Hazrat Yaʿqūb was leaving Faniʾīl, the sun was just rising. Because his hip was injured, he was limping. (For this reason Banī Isrāʾīl does not eat the hip tendon even today.)
