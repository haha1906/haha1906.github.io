Hazrat Yūsuf wept and threw himself on his father. He hugged and kissed his face. Then he called doctors to embalm the corpse. (It would take 40 days in Miṣr to embalm a corpse.) They mourned 70 days in Miṣr for Yaʿqūb (a.s.) When the days of mourning were over, Yūsuf (a.s.) said to Firʿawn's officials, "Please go and tell Firʿawn for me: my father made me swear an oath and told me, 'when I die, bury me in the place where my ancestors are in Kanʿān.' Now if you give me permission, I want to take my father and bury him. After the burial I will return." Firʿawn agreed and said, "Let him go, let him fulfil the promise he had given to his father."
So Hazrat Yūsuf and his brothers took their families and went on the journey to bury their father. (All the young children and animals were left in al-Sadīr.) All the officials of Firʿawn and well-known people of Miṣr also went with them. A big caravan consisting of chariots, horsemen and people went on a journey to Kanʿān. When they came to the other side of the river al-Ardūn, to the threshing place of ʿAwsaj, they mourned and wept for 7 days. The people who lived in ʿAwsaj saw the mourning and named that place Ḥuzn al-Miṣriyyīn (which means, 'the mourning of the Miṣriyyūn'), saying, "The Miṣriyyūn mourned greatly here." The sons of Hazrat Yaʿqūb fulfilled the will of their father: they took him to Kanʿān and buried him in the cave of al-Muḍāʿafah near Mamrā. Then all of them returned to Miṣr.
The Brothers Worry
After the death of their father, the brothers of Yūsuf (a.s.) started worrying, saying: "What if Yūsuf is keeping a grudge in his heart! Will he take revenge from us now?!" Then they sent a message to Hazrat Yūsuf saying: "Before our father died, he gave these instructions: Tell Yūsuf that even though his brothers did evil to him, he should forgive their sin. Please, forgive us! We are also slaves of Allāh whom our father worshipped!" Hearing this, Hazrat Yūsuf's eyes were filled with tears. Then the brothers came themselves and fell before Yūsuf's feet saying, "We are your slaves." Yūsuf said: "Why are you afraid of me? Am I Allāha to judge you or punish you? It is true that you wanted to do evil to me, but Allāh turned your evil intention into something good. He raised me to this position in order to save many many people's lives. Do not be afraid. I will take care of you myself, and your children as well." After this, the brothers' hearts calmed down.
Yūsuf's (a.s.) Death "My Rabb! You have given me authority; You have taught me to interpret dreams. Creator of the heavens and the earth! You are my protector (Waliy ) in this world and in the Hereafter. Let me die a Muslim (in true devotion to you). Join me to the righteous (ṣāliḥīn)." (Yūsuf 12:101).
Hazrat Yūsuf and his brothers stayed in Miṣr. Yūsuf (a.s.) lived to the age of 110. He lived long enough to see the sons and grandchildren of Ifrāyim and the sons of Mākhīr son of Minashshih. Before he died, he said to his brothers: "My death is near. But Allāh will surely help you. He will take you to the land He had promised to our fathers Ibrāhīm, Isḥāq and Yaʿqūb." Then he made them swear an oath, and he said, "When Allāh takes you back to Kanʿān, take my bones with you also." So Yūsuf (a.s.) died at the age of 110. After he died, they embalmed his corpse and put it in a chest in Miṣr.
 
\it The Sūra of Yūsuf in the Qurʾān al-Karīm finishes like this: "This account is part of what was beyond your knowledge. We revealed it to you: you were not present with Yūsuf's brothers when they made their treacherous plans. However eagerly you may want them to, most men will not believe... There are many signs in the heavens and the earth that they pass by and give no heed to – most of them will only believe in Allāh while also joining others with Him. Are they so sure that an overwhelming punishment from Allāh will not fall on them, or that the Last Hour will not come upon them suddenly when they least expect it?" \it (Yūsuf 12:102-103, 105-107).
