The story of Nūḥ (a.s.)a
Many years passed. People increased in number on the earth. As they increased in number, the weight of their sin also increased. Evil was increasing greatly; people were not living in the straight/right path; and all their thoughts were busy with evil. This caused Allāh (s.w.t.) to be very displeased, because the people who were created by Allāh went against the will of Allāh and chose the way of rebellion. Allāh said, "I will destroy mankind from the face of the earth." But Allāh was pleased with the way Nūḥ (a.s.) lived.
Hazrat Nūḥ was loyal (wafāʿ) to Allah's covenant with him and was thankful for Allah's friendship. According to those people who were around him, he lived with taqwā.
Verdict given for Sin
Allāh (s.w.t.) saw that people were corrupted and given to violence. Every inclination of their heart was evil, and all the earth was filled with their unrighteous deeds and oppression. So He said to the prophet Nūḥ: "People have filled the earth with oppression. Now I will destroy all humanity. So I want you to build a boat from cypress wood and to put rooms in it. Then cover it with tar on both the inside and outside. Make a roof for it and leave a 44 centimeter opening below the roof all the way around the boat. Put a door on its side, and build three decks inside the boat—lower, middle, and upper. I will send a flood of water upon the earth. And I will destroy all the people and animals on the earth. But with you I will make a covenant. I will save your family in the boat: you, your wife, your sons and your daughters-in-law". Then Hazrat Nūḥ obeyed all the commands of Allāh (s.w.t.) as he was told.
