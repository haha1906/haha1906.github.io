The Flood
"We sent Nūḥ to his people... but they called him a liar. We saved him, and those who were with him, on the Ark and We drowned those who rejected Our revelations." (Al-Aʿrāf 7:59,64)
Finally, when the time came, Allāh (s.w.t.) said to Nūḥ (a.s.): "Go, take your family and enter the boat. I have not found any righteous men on the earth besides you. Take seven pairs of every halal (clean) animal and bird with you, and one pair from the unclean animals—a male and female of each—so that the birds and animals will survive. In seven days from now, I will send rain for forty days and forty nights on the earth. And I will wipe all the living beings that I have created on the earth". Nūḥ (a.s.) did all the commands of Allāh (s.w.t.) as he was told. He took his wife,a sons and his daughters-in-law and got on the boatb in order to be saved from the flood. As Allāh had said, big and small animals and birds of every kind came to Hazrat Nūḥ in pairsc—male and female—and entered the boat. After that, Allāh closed the door of the boat. 11 Then .... the flood began! All the springs in the earth gushed out. The gates of the sky were opened, 12 and strong rain fell on the earth for forty days and forty nights. d
23 At this time Hazrat Nūḥ was sitting inside the boat with his family: his wife, his sons Shīm, Ḥām, Yāfit, and their wives. 6 At that time Hazrat Nūḥ was six hundred years old.
Did all the rest die? "So We opened the gates of the sky with torrential water, burst the earth with gushing springs: the waters met for a preordained purpose. We carried him along on a vessel of planks and nails." (Al-Qamar 54:11-13).
The flood of waters continued for forty days. As the water increased, it raised the boat high above the ground. Eventually the level of the water became so high that even the highest mountains were below the water. There was so much water that it rose fifteen dhirāʿs e above the peaks of the mountains. Everything that lives on the ground died: animals, birds and humans. Indeed, all the creatures that breathe air perished. Allāh destroyed all life on the land — from humans to animals, from moving creatures on the ground to birds that fly on the sky. Only Hazrat Nūḥ and those who went into the boat with him were left alive.

But they preferred oppression and violence. They would not abandon their evil ways. They did not want to worship Allāh, but instead wanted to defy Him.
