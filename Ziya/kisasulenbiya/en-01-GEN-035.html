The renewal of Faith
At that time a revelation was sent down from Allāh (s.w.t.): "Rise, go to Bītʾīl. There slaughter sacrifices to Me." Hearing this Yaʿqūb (a.s.) told his household and his servants: "Get ready, change your clothes. Get rid of your pagan idols.a We are going to Bītʾīl. We will do ibāda to Allāh who supported me in my difficult days. He has helped me wherever I went." So he took all of his household and his servants, and went on a journey to do ibāda. As they were leaving, nobody from the towns and villages nearby dared to pursue them, because Allāh had put fear into their hearts. After arriving at Bītʾīl, Allāh (s.w.t.) said to Yaʿqūb (a.s.): “I am Allāh who is able to do everything (al-Qadir). A nation will come into existence from you, even a community of nations! Kings will come into the world from your naṣl. Be fruitfulb and multiply. I have given you the land your grandfather Ibrāhīm and your father Isḥāq inherited. It will belong to you and to your descendants after you."
Then Yaʿqūb (a.s.) left Bītʾīl and went on a journey toward Ifrāt.c On the way, the time came for Rāḥīl to have a baby and she gave a birth to her second son — Binyāmin. But the delivery was difficult and painful for her and she died. Yaʿqūb (a.s.) buried her there, on the road to Ifrāt. They kept travelling and finally they arrived at the place where Isḥāq (a.s.) lived, in the city of Ḥibrūnd. (Hazrat Ibrāhīm also lived there before.) Isḥāq (a.s.) lived 180 years and when he died, his sons ʿIsāw and Yaʿqūb (a.s.) buried him together.
