"You will see for sure what happens to Firʿawn," said Allāh (s.w.t.). "He will not only set you free, but he will force you to leave Miṣr. I made a covenant with Ibrāhīm, Isḥāq and Yaʿqūb, and promised to give the land of Kanʿān to their descendants.a Now go and tell Banī Isrāʾīl that I am al-Qayyūm. I will rescue you from slavery with My great power and with My miracles. You will be My people, and I will be your Allāh. Then you will be convinced that I am al-Qayyūm. I will lead you out of Miṣr and bring you to the place I had promised." Mūsā (a.s.) told the people of Isrāʾīl what Allāh (s.w.t.) had said to him. But the ʿIbrāniyyūn had become very discouraged because of the heavy labor and beating thay received, and they did not listen to him.
The staff of Mūsā (a.s.) turns into a snake "So Mūsā threw his staff and – lo and behold! – it was a snake, clear to all, and then he pulled out his hand and – lo and behold! – it was white for all to see." (Al-Aʿrāf 7:107-108)
Then Allāh (s.w.t.) said to Mūsā (a.s.): "Go and tell Firʿawn again to let Banī Isrāʾīl go.
