'A great fish swallowed him'
Al-Ṣāffāt 37:142
Yūnus wrote this poem about his du‘wa inside the fish:
 
In my distress I called out to my Rabb
and Allāh Taʿāla answered me.
I cried out for help from the darkness of the gravea
and you heard my voice.
You threw me into the deepest part of the sea
and its currents swirled around me.
All your breakers and waves washed over me.
I had despaired, thinking,
When death drives me from Your face,
how will I ever make ziyāra to al-Aqsa b again?
And then the waters closed over me,
rushing down my throat;
The dark deep wrapped around me;
Seaweed trapped my head.
I sank down to the roots of the mountains
into barzakh, c
Its gates should have shut behind me forever.
But You brought my life back from its grave.
Subhāna Allāh!
When I was sinking into the sleep of death,
I made zikr d about You.
Ya Allāh! My du‘was reached your throne
Those who seek benefit from worthless idols
abandon the deliverance of Allāh Taʿāla’s never failing love.
But I will make sacrifices to you with a voice of thanksgiving.
I will carry out the obedience I vowed to you.
*Deliverance belongs to Allāh !*
