"Now cut two stone tablets like the first ones, and I will write My laws on them again and give to you. Tomorrow in the morning be ready. Early in the morning come up to My presence, to the mountain Saynāʾ," He said. Mūsā (a.s.) prepared the tablets and the next day early in the morning he got up and went up to the mountain Saynāʾ— to the presence of Allāh (s.w.t.).
Allāh (s.w.t.) descended to the mountain in a bright cloud. As He passed near Mūsā (a.s.), He said: "I am Allāh, Allāh, raḥmān and raḥīm, slow to anger, God who is rich to love and faithfulness.a All of those who love Me, will find mercy and their sins will be forgiven. But evil-doers will for sure receive punishment. I will punish their children up to 3d or 4th generation for the sins of the fathers."
The light showering from the face of the messenger of Allāh
For 40 days and 40 nights nabī Mūsā took lessons on the mountain, in the presence of Allāh. For 40 days he did not eat or drink anything. After forty days he came down to the people with the tablets where the covenant-book was written. His face was shining because he spoke with Allāh (s.w.t.). But he himself was not aware of it. The people were afraid to come close to him, seeing the light coming from his face.
Nabī Mūsā called them to himself. Then he told them all the laws that Allāh had given. After he finished speaking, he covered his face with a veil. So, whenever he went to the presence of Allāh (s.w.t.), he would take the veil from his face, then coming to Banī Isrāʾīl he would explain the commandments he got from Allāh. During each conversation the nation of Isrāʾīl witnessed that his face was shining. Then Mūsā (a.s.) would put a veil again on his face until he went to the presence of Allāh (s.w.t.).
When entering the promised land...
One of the things that Allāh (s.w.t.) had ordered to prophet Mūsā was about the promised land: "I will give you the land of Kanʿān taking it away from the Amūribbūn, Kanʿāniyyūn, Ḥittiyyūn, Farizziyyūn, Ḥawiyyūn and Yabūsiyyūn. But you should obey My orders. Do not make covenants with any of the local people. Otherwise, you will also repeat their sinful deeds. Destroy their altars, and break down the stones they worship. I am jealous for My own people, I do not want them worshipping idols. Do not make any treaties with these peoples. When they worship their gods, they will call you also to engage in idolatry. Then you will betray Me, and repeat the things they are doing. If you take their girls to your sons, your sons will turn their faces away from Me and will give their hearts to the idols that their wives worship."
