Lūṭ (a.s.) separates from Nabī Ibrāhīm
Both Hazrat Ibrāhīm and Lūṭ (a.s.), who was traveling with him, had many flocks and herds and servants. One day, the land became too small for all their animals to graze together. A quarrel stirred up between the shepherds of Ibrāhīm (a.s.) and Lūṭ (a.s.). (The Kanʿāniyyūn and al-Farizziyyūn were also living there at that time.) Then Ibrāhīm said to Lūṭ: "We are brothers to each other, there should never be a quarrel between us or between our shepherds. There is plenty of land! Let us separate. If you stay here, I will move to another place. If you leave, I will stay here."
Hazrat Lūṭ chooses Sidūm
Lūṭ (a.s.) looked toward the city of Sidūm, at the valley of Ardūn, and he liked what he saw. These lands had plenty of water, like the lands of Miṣr. "I choose the Ardūn valley," said Hazrat Lūṭ. And so he separated from Ibrāhīm (a.s.), taking his animals and servants. He moved there and started living near the towns of the Ardūn Valley. He put up his tents near Sidūm. (The people of Sidūm were bad people who were devoted to sinful deeds. They were very rebellious against Allāh.) We will narrate later how these events ended.
