Rāḥīl was not able to have childen, and she was jealous of her sister. One day she even argued with her husband saying: "Give me a child, otherwise I will die!" "What are you saying? Am I Allah? I can't give you children!" said Yaʿqūb (a.s.) angrily. Then Rāḥīl said: "Well, then sleep with my maidservant and let her bear children for me. That way I want can become a mother too." So she gave her maidservant Bilhā to her husband as a wife. Bilhā became pregnant and bore a son. Rāḥīl named the child Dān, saying: "Allāh has judged in my favor. He heard my prayers and gave me a child." Then Bilhā became pregnant a second time and bore another son to Yaʿqūb (a.s.). This time Rāḥīl named the boy Naftālīa, saying: "I struggled hard with my sister and have won."
Since Liʾā had ceased bearing children, she also gave her maidservant Zilfā to her husband as a wife. Zilfā became pregnant and bore a son. Liʾā named the boy Ghādb, saying: "My fortune has come!" Then Zilfā bore another son. Liʾā said: "I am so happy! Now the women will call me happy", and named the child Āshīrc.
During the wheat harvest season, Raʾūbin brought a bunch of love-grass from the wilderness and gave it to his mother Liʾā. Then Rāḥīl said to Liʾā: "Please give me also some of the love-grass your son found." Liʾā said: "Is not it enough that you took away my husband? Now do you want to take away the love-grass that my son brought?" "Ok," said Rāḥīl, "Give me the love-grass, and in return you will lay with our husband tonight." In the evening when Yaʿqūb (a.s.) returned from the wilderness, Liʾā came out to meet him and said: "Today you will sleep with me. I have bought you for one night with the love-grass my son found." Yaʿqūb (a.s.) slept that night with Liʾā and Liʾā became pregnant and bore a son to Yaʿqūb the fifth time. Allāh had accepted Liʾā 's prayers. Then Liʾā named her son Yissākhārd saying "Allāh has rewarded me because I gave my maidservant to my husband". Then she became pregnant again and bore a son to Yaʿqūb (a.s.) the sixth time. And she said: "This is Allāh's gift to me. I bore six sons to my husband. Now he will value me", and she named him Zībūlūne. Then she bore a daughter and named her Dīnah.
Finally Rāḥīl's prayers were also answered: she became pregnant and bore a son. She said: "Allāh has taken away my disgrace. May He give me another son in addition to this." So she named the child Yūsuff.
Labān and Yaʿqūb (a.s.) make an agreement about work
After the birth of Yūsuf, Hazrat Yaʿqūb said to his father-in-law: "Now give me permission so I can return to my father's land. I will take my wives and children also. You know how much I served you for them!" Labān said, "Do not leave. Allāh (s.w.t.) blessed me because of you. See, I have become rich. It would be better if you stay. Tell me, and I will pay you for your work, whatever is necessary." "You know very well how I served you, and how I took care of your sheep and cattle," said Hazrat Yaʿqūb, "You did not have this many animals before I came. After I came, Allāh has blessed you very much. Your flocks and herds increased very much. I should also think about my own family needs now!" Labān said, "How much do you want me to pay you?" Yaʿqūb replied, "I do not need your money. But, I will take care of your sheep further anyway. I have only one condition: today we'll go and separate from the flocks all the speckled, spotted and black sheep, and the speckled and spotted goats. They will be my payment. Afterwards, if you see any white sheep in my flock, or a goat which is not speckled or spotted, then you have the right to say that I have stolen it." "I agree. Let it be as you say", said Labāng and that very day he went and separated the speckled, spotted and black sheep, and the speckled and spotted goats and told his sons: "You will take care of Yaʿqūb's sheep. Take the flock a three day journey away". So Yaʿqūb (a.s.) continued to shepherd his uncle's sheep.
While he was taking care of sheep, here is what he did: He cut some branches from almond trees, poplar trees and plane trees. He peeled off part of the bark to make white streaks. Then he put these branches next to the troughs where the animals drank water. When the animals came to drink, they used to mate in front of the branches, and then when they bore, the kid animals were born speckled or spotted. Yaʿqūb (a.s.) would separate them, and would turn the heads of other sheep/goats toward them when breeding. Whenever big and strong sheep mated, he would put the branches near the troughs, in front of the sheep, but when weak animals mated, he would not put the branches there. As a result, the animals belonging to Lābān were born weak, but the animals belonging to Yaʿqūb (a.s.) were born strong. In this way, Hazrat Yaʿqūb became very rich and owned many flocks, camels and donkeys, as well as male and female slaves.
