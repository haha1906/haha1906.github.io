So Mūsā and Hārūn (a.s.) went back to Firʿawn. Just like Allāh (s.w.t.) had said, Firʿawn demanded a miracle. Then Hārūn (a.s.) threw his staff down on the ground before Firʿawn. The staff immediately turned into a snake.a When he saw this miracle, Firʿawn called inb his scholars and magicians. The magicians of the Miṣriyyūn also threw their staffs to the ground and made them turn into snakes. But Hārūn's snake was stronger than theirs. It swallowedc all the staffs that the Miṣriyyūn had thrown down (which had turned into snakes). Even then Firʿawn acted stubborn and did not let Banī Isrāʾīl go, just as Allāh (s.w.t.) had warned Mūsā (a.s.) from the beginning.d
7 At that time Mūsā (a.s.) was eighty years old, and Hārūn (a.s.) was eighty three years old.
The first plague
"The heart of Firʿawn is still like stone, and he does not want to let My people go," said Allāh (s.w.t.) to Mūsā (a.s.). "Go meet him tomorrow morning on the bank of al-Nīl when he comes out to the river. Take your staff with you, the one that turned into a snake. Tell him: "Allāh (s.w.t.) sent me to you again. He ordered me to tell you, 'Let My people go.' But you have not obeyed yet. So now Allāh (s.w.t.) is going to send a plague to your land. I will hit the river Nīl with my staff, and the river will turn into blood. Then you will be convinced about Allāh's power. As soon as you touch the water with the staff, the water will turn into blood. All the fish will die, the water will stink and the Miṣriyyūn will not be able to drink even a drop from the river. Then tell Hārūn to stretch the staff over all the rivers, canals, ditches and water reservoirs in Miṣr. Then the water in them, and even the water in jugs, will turn into blood".
Nabī Mūsā did as Allāh (s.w.t.) had commanded. The river Nīl turned into blood. The fish died and the water stank. The Miṣriyyūn could not drink the water from the river. All over Miṣr the water reservoirs turned into blood. Then the magicians of Miṣr also turned water into blood with their magic. When Firʿawn saw this, he again made his heart like stone and as Allāh had warned from the beginning, he did not let Banī Isrāʾīl go. (He did not even pay attention to Allāh's power.) Then the Miṣriyyūn began to dig wells along the bank of the river to find drinking water, because blood was flowing in the river instead of water.
