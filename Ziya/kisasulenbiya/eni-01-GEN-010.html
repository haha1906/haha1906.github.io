The Descendants of Nūḥ (a.s.) "We sent Nūḥ and Ibrāhīm, and gave prophethood and scripture to their offspring." (al-Ḥadīd 57:26).
As it was said above, Hazrat Nūḥ had three sons who were saved in the flood: Shīm, Ḥām, Yāfit. 32 All the peoplea who live on the earth came from these three sons of Nūḥ (a.s.).b
From Yāfit came the children named Jūmir, Maʾjūj, Māhāt, Yūnān, Tūbāl, Māshik and Tīrās. And from Jūmir the following children were born: al-Saqālibah, Rīfāth and Tūjarmat. Yūnān had sons called Alīshah, Tarshīsh, and descendents who are called the peoples of Qubruṣ and Aḍanah.
Ḥām had these sons: Ḥabashah, Miṣr, Tafut and Kanʿān. To Ḥabashah the following sons were born: Sabaʾ, Zuwaylah, Zajawah, Raʿamah, Nimrūd and Damas. To Raʿamah were born Shabaʾ and Didān. Later Nimrūd became the king of the large territory that included the land of Shīnūr, the cities of Babil, Karak, Karas and Kalnah. The descendants of Miṣr were(the people ofc ) Banī Lūd, Iskandarān, Lahāb, Naftūḥ, Fatrūs, Dimyāṭ and Ṣaʿīd (from whom the Qawm al-Jālūt came). And from Kanʿān came Ṣīdūn and Ḥīt, and (the people of) Yabūs, Amūr, Jirjāsh, Ḥawi, ʿArq, al-Sīniyya, Arwād, Ḥimṣ and Ḥamāw. Later these tribes, the descendants of Kanʿān, took the territory from Ṣīdūn to Ghazzah in the direction of al-Khilūṣ, and from there to the towns of Sidūm, ʿAmūrah, Admah, Ṣibūyim and Lashʿ.
The sons of Shīm were: Khuzukhistān, Mawṣil, Arfakhshād, Lūd and Arman. Arman had sons named al-Ghūṭah, Ḥūlah, Gharāmiqa and Miyāsh. To Arfakhshād was born a son named Shālaḥ. Shālaḥ had a son called ʿĪbir. ʿĪbir had two sons. To the first he gave the name Qāsim (which means separation), because the people on the eath were separated at that time. The name of his second son was Qaḥṭān. From Qaḥṭān were born the sons Almūdād, Shālif, Ḥaḍramawt, Yāriḥ, Hadūrām, Uzāl, Diqlah, Farimiyyīna, Abīmāʾīl, Sabā, Ūfīr, Zuwaylah and Yūbāb. They lived in the area that included Makkah and al-Madīnah, in the Eastern hill country.
