Yūnus AS
"Yūnus too was one of the messengers" Al-Ṣāffāt 37:139
 
This is the story of how Allāh (s.w.t.) sent Nabī Yūnus to make daʿwa to the Nināwiyyun. Nabī Yūnus belonged to the Banī Yāqūb\ft and was a part of the line of prophets that came after *sayyidunā* Ibrāhīm (a.s.). Like all the prophets, Allāh (swt) guided him to the straight path (ṣirāt al-mustaqīm).\xdc He is also called Dhū al-nūn and Ṣāhib al-ḥūt, which both mean the 'companion of the fish'.\xdc Allāh (s.w.t.) says, 'And remember the man with the whale …'.\xdc
The Nināwiyyun were a violent and cruel people, and were enemies of the Banī Yāqūb. They practiced shirk and worshipped false gods. Yūnus thought that Allāh (s.w.t.) should destroy their city, but Allāh is al-Raḥmān, the Merciful.⁠a They did not realise that the evil they were doing would bring disaster on them. Neither did they know about Allāh (s.w.t.)'s promise: "There is good news (al-bushrā) for those who reject the worship of false gods and turn to Allāh (in repentance)." ⁠b So He sent Nabī Yūnus (a.s.) to them and oﬀered them the chance to repent from their evil practices.
'He fled to the overloaded ship'
Al-Ṣāffāt 37:140
One day Yūnus ibn Amittay received Allah (s.w.t.)'s command: 'Go at once to the important city of Nināwā. I am concerned about the disasterc that their sin is bringing upon them.d So go there and warn them.' Instead, Yūnus (a.s.) intended to fleee in the opposite direction toward the open sea to get away from al-Quds.f Yūnus thought that he could escape Allāh’s command by going where people didn’t worship or obey Allāh. He went to the port city of Yāfā and found a ship there that was going out to sea. He paid the fare and went down into the ship so that he could go with them out to sea—to a place where people didn’t worship Allāh.
Once the ship was out to sea, Allāh sent a strong wind that caused a terrible storm to hurl waves against every side of the ship. The sailors felt that there was no escape and became very afraid because it looked like the ship was going to break apart.g Each man cried out to his god for help. Then they began throwing the cargo overboard to lighten the ship so that it wouldn’t sink. But Yūnus had gone down into the hold of the ship to lie down and had fallen sound asleep there.
When the captain of the boat found Nabī Yūnus sleeping in the bottom of the boat, he was very surprised. He said to him, 'How can you be in such a deep sleep? Get up! Call out to your god!h Maybe your god will hear your prayers and have mercy on us so that we won't perish!'
They cast lots and he suffered defeat
Al-Ṣāffāt 37:141
The storm was getting worse so the sailors said to one another, 'Let’s cast lots to find out whose wrongdoing has caused the gods to bring this disaster (calamity) on us'. So they cast lots and the lot fell on Yūnus. In desperation they began asking him questions to find out what was going on:
'Is this disaster (calamity) your fault or ours?!?
 
... What kind of work do you do?
… Where did you come from?
… What country are you from?
… Who are your people?'
He finally had to tell them: 'I am a Prophet from the Banī Isrāʾīl. I fear and worship Allāh who made the sea and the dry land'. When they heard this, they trembled with fear and cried out, 'What have you done?'i
When the storm raged even more, they asked Yūnus, 'What should we do with you so that the sea will become still?' Yūnus answered them and said, 'I know that this great storm has come against you because of me. The lot did not fall on me by chance. So pick me up and throw me into the sea! Then it will stop raging against you.'
But they were afraid to harm Nabī Yūnus, so they tried instead to row back to the shore. No matter how hard they tried, they got nowhere because the storm was raging against them. After trying everything they could, they cried out to Allāh : 'Ya Allāh! You have acted according to Your will! Please don't let us die because of this man’s life!j Don’t hold us responsible for this man's blood!' Then they picked up the Prophet and hurled him into the sea.
*Imagine!* The sea suddenly stopped raging against them! After this the sailors deeply feared Allāh and offered a sacrificek to Him when they got back on land. They repented and made vows to Allāh.
Meanwhile Allāh had provided a big fish to swallow Yūnus. And he was in the belly of this fish for three days and three nights.
