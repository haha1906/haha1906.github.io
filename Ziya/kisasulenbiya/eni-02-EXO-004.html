The miracles Allāh (s.w.t.) gave Mūsā (a.s.) "(Mūsā) threw it down and– lo and behold!– it became a fast-moving snake. He said, ‘Pick it up without fear: We shall turn it back into its former state. Now place your hand under your armpit and it will come out white, though unharmed.’" (Tā Hā 20:20-22)
Then Hazrat Mūsā asked, "What will I do if Banī Isrāʾīl do not believe me or do not listen to me?" "What is in your hand?" said Allāh. "A walking stick," said Mūsā (a.s.). "Throw it on the ground!" said Allāh. Mūsā (a.s.) threw the staff on the ground. The staff immediately turned into snake! "When (Mūsā) saw it moving like a snake, he turned and fled. (Then We said,) 'Mūsā, do not be afraid...'" (Al-Naml 27:10)
"Reach out and grab its tail," said Allāh (s.w.t.). When Mūsā (a.s.) grabbed the tail of the snake, the snake turned back into a staff! Allāh said, "After seeing this miracle, the people will believe that indeed Ia sent you. Now put your hand inside your robe!" Nabī Mūsā put his hand inside his robe. When he took it out, he saw that his hand was white, like the hand of people with vitiligo! "Put your hand inside your robe again!" said Allāh (s.w.t.). Mūsā (a.s.) put his hand inside his robe a second time. When he took it out, he saw that his hand was back to its previous state. "If people do not believe the first miracle, show them the second one," said Allāh (s.w.t.). "If some people do not believe the second miracle either, then take water from the river Nīl and pour it on the dry ground. The water you take from the river will become red blood on the ground."
Mūsā (a.s.) asks for a helper
"My Allāh, I have never been very good with words", said Hazrat Mūsā (a.s.). "Even now you have said so many words, but I can not deliver them to Banī Isrāʾīl. "Lord, lift up my heart and ease my task for me. Untie my tongue, so that they may understand my words." b Allāh (s.w.t.) said, "Who gave man a tongue? Who makes him mute, deaf, or able to see or blind? So go now. I Myself will help you; I will teach you what to say." "Oh my Rabb, okay, send me, but give me another man as a helper," nabī Mūsā begged. c
 
He had doubt about himself. Though this stirred up the anger of Allāh, his request was accepted. He said, "O Mūsā, your request is granted." d "Your brother Hārūn will become a speaker for you. You will tell him what to say, and he will speak on behalf of you in front of the people. He will deliver your words to the people. I will help you both. I will teach you what needs to be done. Now get going. Take the staff with you too, and use it to perform miracles".e
 
At that time Allāh (s.w.t.) called the nabī Mūsā to His service. He may call you also to His service. How will you answer? Will you obey? Or will you refuse his service? We human beings are the servants of Allāh (s.w.t.), and we should serve Allāh (s.w.t.). The original meaning of "ibādāt" is — "to serve".
 
Mūsā (a.s.) returns to Miṣr "Out of Our grace We granted him his brother Hārūn as a nabī (so that he would be his helper)." (Maryam 19:53)
After that Mūsā (a.s.) went to his father-in-law and asked his permissionf to leave. After his father-in-law gave him a blessing, he put his wife and children on a donkey, and he took the staff in his hand which Allāh (s.w.t.) had commanded him to take, and departed for Miṣr. Sir Hārūn met his brother Mūsā at Jabal Allāh. Then Mūsā (a.s.) told his brother everything Allāh (s.w.t.) had said, and about all the signs/miracles he was to perform in Miṣr. When they arrived in Miṣr they called all elders of Banī Isrāʾīl, and Hārūn (a.s.) delivered the words of Allāh (s.w.t.) to them. Then the nabī Mūsā gave proof for the words he spoke by showing them the signs/miracles. The people believed. And all of them put their heads on the ground to worship when they heard that Allāh (s.w.t.) saw their sufferings, and was going to help them.
