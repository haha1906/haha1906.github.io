The Covenant is tested again "It was a test to prove [their true characters]" (Al-Ṣaffāt 37:106).
The last test which came to Ibrāhīm (a.s.)
Quite some time passed in between. One day Allāh wanted to test prophet Ibrāhīm's faithfulness to the covenant and said: "Ibrāhīm!" Ibrāhīm (a.s.) answered: labbayk ("Yes, sir!"). Allāh said, "Take your son and go to the land you have seen.a Sacrifice him for Me there on a mountain I will tell you about."
 
Let us imagine that you also have a son whom you dearly love and who was born in your old age. So now, if Allāh tells you to sacrifice that son, for whom you prayed for 25 years, how would you feel? What did Ibrāhīm (a.s.) feel at that time?
He loved his son very much. And he was confused, thinking: "Allāh loves my son too, doesn't He? Then why did He command me to sacrifice him?"
"Maybe Allāh wants to raise him from the dead? Of course, Allāh is able to do that", he said to himself with faith. "But what if He does not resurrect him? Then what happens?" Thoughts like this tormented him, but in the end, Nabī Ibrāhīm decided to trust Allāh and obey the decision of the Creator.
Obedience
Ibrāhīm (a.s.) got up early the next day and saddled his donkey. He chopped some wood to burn the sacrifice and took two of his servants and his sonb, and started a journey toward the place Allāh had told him. On the third day, the place they were going was visible in the distance. Then Hazrat Ibrāhīm told his servants: "You wait here. We will go there and after doing ʿibāda we'll return." Then he put the wood which he had chopped for burning the sacrifice onto his son's shoulder, and he himself took a knife and a flint to start a fire, and they went on in the direction of the mountain.
"Dear father!" said his son on the way. "Yes, my son?" said İbrahim (a.s.) "We have a flint and wood to make fire," said his son, "but where is the sheep to slaughter as sacrifice!?" Ibrāhīm (a.s.) said, "Allāh Himself will provide the sheep for the sacrifice, my son."
Allāh Himself will provide "When the boy was old enough to work with his father, Ibrāhīm said, ‘My son, I have seen myself sacrificing you in a dream. What do you think?’ He said, ‘Father, do as you are commanded. Inshāʾallāh, you will find me steadfast.’" (Al-Ṣaffāt 37:102).
After arriving the place Allāh had said, Hazrat Ibrāhīm made a sacrifice-place and put wood on it. Then his son gave his hands to be tied and Ibrāhīm laid him on the wood. Then he took the knife in his hand to sacrifice him. Then suddenly a voice came from the sky: "Ibrāhīm! Ibrāhīm!" "Yes, sir", said Ibrāhīm (a.s.). "Do not touch the boy!" said the voice. "Now I know that you are a man of taqwā, because you did not hold back even your beloved son from Me."
Hazrat Ibrāhīm looked up and saw a ram. Its horns were caught in the bushes. He went and brought the ram and he slaughtered it instead of his son and performed the rite of the Ahd sacrifice. (Later on the prophet Ibrāhīm named this place "Allāh provides". This is why there is a saying among some people that "Allāh will Himself provide on His mountain.")
Comment about burning the sacrifices
May be someone can ask: Did people burn the sacrifices devoted to Allāh at that time? Why did Ibrāhīm (a.s.) put wood on the altar first?
This sacrifice was called а burning sacrifice \it in the Scripture (see ĀlʿImrān 3:183) and when it was done the animal (being sacrificed) was given to Allāh (s.w.t.) fully/completely. No part of it was spared. This meant that a man who was presenting the sacrifice was giving himself completely to the will of Allāh through this sacrifice.
What is more expensive: a ram or a son?
Hazrat Ibrāhīm's son was meant to die. But Allāh gave a ram in his place. Ibrāhīm (a.s.) brought the ram and slaughtered it in his son's place.
There is a verse in Qurʾān al-Karīm about this: "We ransomed his son with a great sacrifice." c \it What do you think: which one is worth more, a son or a ram? Of course, a son is worth more! You would probably never exchange your son for a thousand rams. Then why did Allāh say "We ransomed his son with a great sacrifice"?
The reason is that the ram Allāh (s.w.t.) gave was a symbol of a Great Sacrifice that was going to be given in the future. What sacrifice is that? We are not perfect. Our obedience to Allāh is not perfect. This is why humanity is in need of the great sacrifice given by Allāh. Sacrifices are made to show our devotion to Allah (s.w.t.), but our sacrifices are never given with a pure heart. Allah (s.w.t.)'s great sacrifice makes a way for us to participate in the blessings and promises of the covenant.
All nations will get blessing through your descendant
At that time Allāh said to Hazrat Ibrāhīm: "I swear by My own name that because you trusted Me and did not spare your beloved son from Me, I will surely bless you; I will make your descendants as numerous as the stars in the sky and the sand on the beach. Because you obeyed Me, I will give blessing to the all nations of the earth through your dhurriyya."
 
\it Allāh (s.w.t.) said: "I will give blessing through your descendant to all the nations of the earth." Here a special blessing was promised — the blessing of Allah's raḥmathat will rescue us from our spiritual wrongdoing. Allāh (s.w.t.) promised that Ādam (a.s.)'s wrongdoing would result in a world where the Shaytan fights mankind and where people fight one another and commit sins. However one of the descendants of Ibrāhīm would bring forgiveness to the earth. And this forgiveness has power because of Ibrāhīm's Friendship Covenant (Ahd) with Allāh. And those who enter the covenant will inherit \wa Janna. As it is written in Sūrah al-Muʾminūn: "They are faithful to their pledges and covenants. They keep up their prayers. They will be the heirs who inherit \wa Firdaws as their own, there to remain (forever)." \it (Al-Muʾminūn 23:8-11).
The Faith of Ibrāhīm (a.s.)
Hazrat Ibrāhīm believed in the promises of Allāh. Even though Allāh had said: "Your descendants will continue through your son", he took his son to sacrifice him. He did it because he firmly believed that Allāh was able to resurrect his son from the dead. It is easier to say this than to actually take you own son to kill him. Who can do that? Only a man who trusts in Allāh's goodness. If you want to submit yourself to Allāh, you first need to have a good opinion of him and believe that He is good. Whoever wants to come closer to Allāh, he must believe that Allāh rewards those who search after Him. \it Search for Allāh. Those who search for Allāh will never be deprived of Allāh's raḥma.
