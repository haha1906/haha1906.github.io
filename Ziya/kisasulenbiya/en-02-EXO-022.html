If someone steals someone's ox and sells it or slaughters it, he should pay back five oxen for each ox stolen, and four sheep for each sheep stolen.
If the stolen animal is caught alive, he must pay back double the value of the animal. If he can not pay it, sell the thief himself as a slave.
If a thief is struck and killed while he was breaking into somebody's house at night, the man who kills him is not guilty of murder, but if this killing happens during daytime, the man who kills him will be considered a murderer.
If someone allows his animal to go into a field or garden and damage someone else's crops, the owner of the animal should pay him from the best of his own fruit or crops.
If someone starts a fire, and it happens that someone else's harvest or crops are burned unexpectedly, the man who started the fire should pay restitution for it.
If you give money or things to someone as amāna (for safekeeping) and a thief steals it, when the thief is found, he must pay back double. If the thief is not found, then the owner of the house must swear before Allāh (s.w.t.) saying: "I did not do khiyāna to the amāna."
Suppose you give your ox or donkey or sheep or another animal to someone as amana, and the animal dies or gets hurt or is stolen, but there is no witness. In that case, the neighbour must swear before Allāh saying: “I did not do khiyana to your animal”. The owner of the animal should accept his oath and no restitution for the animal is required. But if the animal was stolen, he must pay restitution. If however it was eaten by a wild animal, he must show the remains of the dead animal as evidence. Restitution does not need to be paid in this case either.
If you borrow someone's animal and it gets an injury or dies, you (plural) must pay restitution for the animal. But if the owner of the animal was present when it got the injury, the restitution no restitution needs to be paid. If the animal was rented, then the money you paid for the rent covers the loss or harm.
If a man seduces a virgin who is not engaged, he must pay the dowry for her and marry her. But if the girl's father refuses to give his daughter to him, he must still pay the dowry given for virgins as a restitution.
Death is the punishment for those who practice magic.
Anyone who has sexual relations with an animal is worthy of death.
Do not treat foreigners badly and do not oppress them. Remember that you also were once foreigners in Miṣr.
Do not mistreat widows or orphans. If you do, they will cry out to me and I will definitely listen to their cry, and will be angry against you. Then your wives will become widows, your children will become orphans. So do not hurt them.
Deu 15:7 Be kind and generous towards any of your brothers who are poor. Do not be mean and selfısh. Deu 15:8 If they come asking for a loan, give them enough to cover their need. Do not charge interest like a moneylender.
Deu 15:10 Give generously to the poor, not grudgingly, because then Allāh (s.w.t.) will bless your work.
If you take someone's robe as a pledge, give it back before sunset. This coat may be his only covering to keep warm. If so, then how can he sleep without a covering? I will listen to him when he cries out to me, because I am God of mercy and compassion.
Do not speak bad words against Allāh (s.w.t.). Do not curse the leaders of the nation.
