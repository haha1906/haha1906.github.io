The burning busha
Mūsā (a.s.) was taking care of his father-in-law's sheep. One day he drove the flock across the desert, toward the mountainb which was on Tuwa. There Allāh (s.w.t.) made a fire appear to him. Sir Mūsā saw that a bush was burning, but it was not burned up in the fire! "Wow, that is so strange!" Mūsā said to himself. "Why is the bush burning but not being consumed?"c "Then he said to his people, ‘Stay here––I can see a fire. Maybe I can bring you a flaming brand from it or find some guidance there.’" d So he went closer to the bush.
At that moment a voice came out of the fire: "Mūsā! Mūsā!" "Labbay!" said Mūsā (a.s.). "Stop, do not come any closer! Take off your sandals, because you are on holy ground. I am the Ilāh of your ancestors Ibrāhīm, Isḥāq and Yaʿqūb".e Mūsā (a.s.) was afraid to look at the Light f of Allāh and covered his face.g
"My people are in suffering. They are crying out for help because of their overseers. I am Raḥmān. The time has come to rescue My people from the grip of the Miṣriyyūn. I will lead them to Kanʿān — to a land where there is plenty of honey and milk. Get prepared. I will send you to Miṣr, to Firʿawn. You will take my people, Banī Isrāʾīl, out of Miṣr."
Allāh is al-raḥmān al-raḥīm. He saw that Banī Isrāʾīl was suffering in slavery and he had mercy on them. "Go to Firʿawn, for he has truly become a tyrant." h
"Who am I to go to Firʿawn?!" said Hazrat Mūsā. "How can I take Banī Isrāʾīl out of Miṣr?" "I will be with you, I will support you," said Allāh (s.w.t.). "You (plural) will leave Miṣr and come here. On this mountain you (plural) will worship Me. That will be the proof that I have sent you." Then Mūsā (a.s.) asked, "If Banī Isrāʾīl asks: "Who is our God? What can he do?", what will I say?"i Allāh (s.w.t.) said: "I am al-Qayyūmj. This name of Mine will be remembered for many generations. Go, and gather the elders. Tell them: "Al-Qayyūm — God (literally: ilāh) of our fathers Ibrāhīm and Isḥāq and Yaʿqūb appeared to me and told me this: 'I stand here watching your condition in Miṣr. As I had promised, I will bring you out of this house of suffering and I will take you to a land where there is plenty of honey and milk.'" They will accept your word. Then go to Firʿawn together and tell him this: "Allāh intends to meet with us.k He commanded us to travel three days journey to the desert and offer sacrifices to Him there." But Firʿawn will not give permission for you to go. Then I will demonstrate My power and My miracles. I will shower disasters on the Miṣriyyūn. Then he will be forced to let you go."
