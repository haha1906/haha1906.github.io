Allah (swt) makes an ahd with Ibrāhīm (a.s.)
"Whose religious devotion could be better in religion than those who direct themselves wholly to Allah, remain virtuous, and follow the ḥanīf religion of Ibrāhīm? Allāh took Ibrāhīm as a friend." (Al-Nisāʾ 4:125)
One day Allāh spoke to Hazrat Ibrāhīm: "Leave the house of your father and go to the land that I will show you. I will create a great nation from you. I will give you blessings and make your name great. You will be a source of blessings. I will bless those who bless you, but I will curse those who treat you lightly. Through you, all the tribes of the earth will become part of my chain of blessing."
 
Allāh (s.w.t.) had a desire that all humanity would be restored to the closeness they shared with him on the Day of Alast, so that the khilāfa of Hazrat Ādam would be continued. For this reason He decided to make an ahd (covenant) with Ibrāhīm (a.s.).
The Promise about Blessing
In this way Allāh (s.w.t.) took Ibrāhīm (a.s.) under His protection and said: "All the tribes of the earth will find blessing through you." Do you remember that in the beginning, Ādam (a.s.) and Ḥawwāʾ lived in Janna, in the presence of Allāh? But then they ate the forbidden fruit and went astray from fiṭra. As a result, sin and death and a curse came on the earth so that all mankind is pulled away from the fiṭra of their declaration on the Day of Alast. There was a clear reason that Allāh warned them by saying: "You will die on the same day you eat that fruit!" (Here, the meaning was not just about physical death, but also about spiritual death. In other words, they lost the closeness they experienced with Allāh (s.w.t.) in Janna when they still followed the the ḥanīf religion of their fiṭra.
And now Allāh is giving a promise to Nabī Ibrāhīm to restore the blessing of faith. This blessing will be given to all people groups, that is, all the tribes of the world. If anybody becomes a member of Ibrāhīm's household and makes bayʿah to the ḥanīf religion of their fiṭra, that person will have a part in that same blessing. His sins will be forgiven and he will become one of the people who is close to Allāh. This is why the Qurʾān says: "Follow the \dc \wa ḥanīf\wa* religion of Ibrāhīm." a
 
At that time Hazrat Ibrāhīm was 75 years old. He obeyed Allāh and left Hārān, and went to the land of Kanʿān. He took his wife Sārah b, his nephew Lūṭc (a.s.), his servants and all the wealth he had gained in Hārān. When they arrived in Kanʿān, Allāh (s.w.t.) gave a revelation to Ibrāhīm (a.s.) and said: "I will give all this land to your descendants." (At that time Hazrat Ibrāhīm was still childless.)
 
\it Ibrāhīm (a.s.) is famous among the prophets by the name "Khalilullah", which means "Allāh's friend". He was called this because love for Allāh (s.w.t.) filled every aspect of his being. He trusted Allāh (s.w.t.). And because he trusted Him, he was obeying Him. When Allāh told him to go, he started the journey even though he did not know where he was going. Because of his trust in Allāh, he left and lived as a stranger, away from his people. He lived the life of a nomad for the sake of Allah (s.w.t.). He had high regard (ḥusn al-ẓann) for Allāh because he loved Allāh and knew that he was Allāh's beloved.
Ibrāhīm's (a.s.) trip to Miṣr
Some time later a severe famine started and Nabī Ibrāhīm went to Miṣr to live there temporarily. As they were approaching Miṣr, he said to his wife Sārah: "You are very beautiful. If the Miṣriyyūn learn that I am your husband, they will kill me in order to have you. This is why you should tell them that you are my sister.d Then they will treat me well and will not kille me." Indeed, as soon as they arrived in Miṣr, the beauty of Sārah attracted everybody's attention. When Firʿawn's officials saw her beauty, they toldf Firʿawn about it. Then Sārah was taken to his palace.g
But then...
But Allāh (s.w.t.) inflicted a bad disease on Firʿawn and his palace people, because they brought Sārah to the palace. When Firʿawn understood what was happening, he became afraid and immediately called Hazrat Ibrāhīm to his palace and told him: "Why have you done this? Why didn't you say that Sārah is your wife? Why did you call her "my sister"? If you have told the truth, I would not have brought her to the palace. Go, take your wife, and leave my country." Then he commanded his people: "Take this man to the border of my country, with all of his wealthh and with his wife!" So, Ibrāhīm (a.s.) returned Kanʿān with his wife and with all of his possessions.
