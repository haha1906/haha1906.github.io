Firʿawn's dreamsa
After two years, Firʿawn saw a dream. In his dream he was standing by the river al-Nīl. Suddenly seven fat, healthy cows came out of the river and started grazing along the bank among the reeds. After them, seven ugly, skinny cows came out of the river, went up to the fat cows, and ate them. At this point Firʿawn woke up.
A little later, he went to sleep and saw another dream. In this dream, seven full heads of grain grew up on a single stalk. After that, seven other heads of grain grew out, but they were thin and scorched by the eastern wind. Then those heads with thin grains swallowed up the full heads. At this point Firʿawn woke up again and realized that it had been a dream.
These dreams bothered Firʿawn very much. The next morning he called for all the magicians and wise men of Miṣr to come. But no-one could interpret his dreams. Then the butler of Firʿawn said: "Your Majesty, I must confess today that I have done something wrong. Once you were angry with me and with the baker and you threw us into prison. When we were in prison, one night we both had dreams. Our dreams had different interpretations. There was a young ʿIbrāniy fellow in the prison - a slave of the commander of the guards. We told our dreams to this fellow. He interpreted them. After three days what he had said was fulfilled exactly: I was restored to my previous position, and the baker was hanged on a pole." \it Then he said: "Send me, I will tell you the meaning of this." b, \it and he came to the prison, to Yūsuf (a.s.) and then came back to Firʿawn and gave him the report. Firʿawn sent men to the prison saying: "Bring Yūsuf (a.s.)."
Yūsuf (a.s.) in the presence of Firʿawn "When the messenger came to fetch Yūsuf, he said: "Go back to your master and ask him about what happened to the women who cut their hands... [This was] for my master to know that I did not betray him behind his back." (Yūsuf 12:50,52).
\it Because of that the "crime" of Yūsuf (a.s.) was investigated again, and it was proven that he was without blame.
After that they brought Hazrat Yūsuf quickly from prison. After he had shaved and changed his clothes, he went into the presence of Firʿawn.
"I had a dream," said Firʿawn to him. "But no-one can interpret my dream. They say you are able to interpret dreams when you hear them." "I cannot do this myself," Hazrat Yūsuf said to Firʿawn, "But Allāh can interpret your dream and make your heart peaceful." Then Firʿawn told him the dream he had: "In my dream I was standing on the bank of the river al-Nīl. Suddenly seven fat, healthy cows came out of the river and started grazing among the reeds along the bank. Behind them, seven more ugly, skinny cows came out from the river. I have not seen such bad-looking cows anywhere in Miṣr. Then the skinny cows ate the seven fat cows which had come out previously. But even after eating them, they looked just as bad as before. Then I woke up. In a little while, I saw another dream. In my dream there were seven good heads with full grains growing out of one stalk. Then seven more heads grew up. They were scorched from the eastern wind, with thin grains. And they ate the heads that had full grains. These are the dreams I had. I told them to the magicians, but none of them could interpret them."
The interpretation of the dreams
Yūsuf (a.s.) said to Firʿawn, "Your Majesty, both of your dreams have one meaning. Allāh has made it known to you in advance what He is going to do. The seven good cows and seven good heads are the sign of seven prosperous years. The seven ugly, skinny cows and the seven shriveled heads that come after them is an indicator of a famine that will continue for seven years. Allāh has shown to you what He is going to do in this land: for seven years Miṣr will have an abundant harvest. But then for the next seven years, there will be such a bad famine that everybody will forget the years of abundance. The fact that the dream has been repeated twice means, that Allāh had decided to do this, and will do it soon. Your Majesty, you now find a wise man and put him responsible over the lands of Miṣr. Then send special supervisors all over the country. They should take one fifth of the harvests in Miṣr during the good seven years and store them in the cities. During the following seven years of famine you will use this stored grain. Otherwise Miṣr will be destroyed because of the famine."
Yūsuf (a.s.) gets a new position Once (Firʿawn) had spoken with (Yūsuf), he said: "From now on you will have our trust and favour." (Yūsuf, 12:54).
The king of Miṣr and the palace officials liked the words of Yūsuf (a.s.). Then Firʿawn said to his officials: "Who do you think can do this work better than Yusuf? It is obvious that Allāh has filled him with the spirit of wisdom!" Then he said to Yūsuf (a.s.): "Since Allāh has revealed all this to you, then there is nobody wiser or smarter than you. You will be responsible for this yourself. Starting today, you will be my right hand deputy. From now on, everybody except me will obey you. All Miṣr will obey you. You will now govern the kingdom!" Then he took his royal ring from his finger, and put it on Yūsuf (a.s.)'s finger. He gave him nice clothes to wear made from fine linen, and put a gold chain around his neck. Then he put him on the second royal chariot. Wherever Hazrat Yūsuf (a.s.) went, announcers were shouting in front of his chariot: "Bow down, bow down!". So Firʿawn appointed Hazrat Yūsuf as head over all Miṣr, and married him to a daughter of one of his officials. At that time Yūsuf (a.s.) was 30 years old. "In this way We settled Yūsuf in that land to live wherever he wished." (Yūsuf, 12:56).
The years of abundance
The years of abundance started. For seven years the harvest was good and plenty everywhere. Every year Hazrat Yūsuf took one fifth of the harvest, and set it aside and stored it in the cities. The grain being stored was so much that eventually Yūsuf (a.s.) stopped counting it.
During those years, Hazrat Yūsuf's wife bore two sons. Yūsuf (a.s.) named his oldest son Minashshih, saying "Because I lost my family, Allāh filled the emptiness by giving me a family, and has made me forget the difficulties I had in my life." He named his second son Ifrāyim, saying "Allāh gave success in my difficult life spent in a foreign land."
The Famine Starts
Finally, the period of seven years of abundance came to an end. As Yūsuf (a.s.) had said, the seven years of famine started. There was a famine in the surrounding countries also. As the famine affected Miṣr, the people of Miṣr came to Firʿawn asking for help. Firʿawn told them: "Go to Yūsuf. Do what he says." So, when the famine spread everywhere in Miṣr, Hazrat Yūsuf opened the storehouses and started selling grain to the Miṣriyyūn. When other nations heard that there was grain in Miṣr, they started coming to Yūsuf (a.s.), because the famine was so bad in those places as well.
