A prophecy Yaʿqūb (a.s.) Made about his Sons "(Ibrāhīm) commanded his sons to (devote themselves to Allāh), as did Yaʿqūb: 'My sons, Allāh has chosen (your) religion for you, so make sure you devote yourselves to Him, to your dying moment.'" (Al-Baqara 2:132).
Then Yaʿqūb (a.s.) called all of his sons and said: "My sons, listen, I want to tell you what happens in the future. Raʾūbin! You are my firstborn son. You are the first fruit of my strength and power. In power and position you are higher than everybody. But you are as unstable as the water of the mountain. Because you defiled my honour, I deprive you of the right of the firstborn.
You Samīʿun and Lāwī! You are two of a kind. Both of you are without mercy and unkind. I do not participate in your council, and I do not take part in your evil plans. You killed people by your anger; you enjoyed cutting oxen's ligaments. Curses be on your merciless anger! Curses be on your unkind anger! I will scatter your descendants into all Isrāʾīl.a
Yahūdā, you will conquer your enemies in battles. All of your brothers will praise you; they will bow down before you. You are like a big lion which came back from hunting and is taking a rest. Who can dare to bother him? The royal staff will remain in your hand, until the Wārith comes whom all nations will obey. You will have an abundant life. What you eat will be in front of you; what you are going to eat will be behind you. You won't lack anything; your children will be healthy, strong and beautiful.
Zībūlūn, you will live on the seashore. You will be a port for ships. Your borders will extend toward Ṣīdūn.
Yissākhār, you are strong, but you are ready to work like a donkey for everybody, for the things you like.
Dān, you will govern your tribe yourself. Though your tribe is small, it will punish enemies very well.
Oh Allāh, I am waiting for Your salvation!
Ghād, raiders will attack and conquer you, but later on you will chase them out.
Āshīr! You will have plenty of provisions. You will eat (different kinds of) royal food.
Naftālī! You are like a deer who is free, and whose children are beautiful.
Yūsuf! Like a vine who sprouts its branches to all sides, your descendants also will be many on the earth. Your enemies attacked you and showered arrows on your head, but under the shadow (protection) of Yaʿqūb's mighty Rabb, Isrāʾīl's Shepherd and Mountain, your bow stayed solid, your hands stayed strong. May Allāh of my forefathers help you! May Mighty Allāh bless you! May He shower His blessings on your head from the earth and sky! May He make your children many, and their provisions plenty! Your father's prayers are greater than the eternal mountains. Yūsuf, you are a prince among your brothers; these blessings will be put on your head like crown.
Binyāmin! You are a fierce wolf. In the morning you attack your prey, in the evening you divide your prey."
With these words, Isrāʾīl (a.s.) distributed blessings to his children. Each of the 12 brothers received a blessing appropriate to his character. The 12 tribes of Isrāʾīl, the Banī Isrāʾīl came into existence from these 12 brothers.
The Burial of Hazrat Yaʿqūb Were you there to see when death came upon Yaʿqūb? When he said to his sons,"What will you worship after I am gone?" they replied, "We shall worshop your Allāh, the Allāh of your fathers Ibrāhīm, Ismāʿīl and Isḥāq, one single Allāh: we devote ourselves (we are muslims) to Him" (Al-Baqara 2:133)
Then Yaʿqūb (a.s.) gave his dying wishes to his sons: "Now I am leaving this world. I will go and be added to the line of my ancestors. Bury me next to my fathers, in the cave of al-Muḍāʿafah in Kanʿān. My grandfather Ibrāhīm had bought that place together with its field from ʿEfrūn al-Ḥittiy. Your grandfathers Ibrāhīm and Isḥāq, as well as your grandmothers Sārah and Ribqah were buried there. I also buried Liʾā there.b My grandfather Ibrāhīm bought that cave together with its field for that purpose from the Ḥittiyyūn." After giving these instructions to his sons, he laid down on his bed and died.
