The Story of Ādam (a.s.)\mt*
 
As it is written in the Qurʾān al-Karīm, "Allāh Almighty (s.w.t.) created Ādam (a.s.) from clay." (Ṣād 38:71).
The Creation of Ādam (a.s.)
Allāh made a man from the soil and breathed life-giving breath from His spirita into the man's nostrils, and the man started breathing.b At that time there was no-one else on the earth besides Ādam (a.s.). Allāh made a garden in a place called ʿAdn and filled it with beautiful and delicious fruit trees. There were two special trees in the middle of the garden: one was the tree of life, and the second one was the tree of knowing good and evil. So Allāh (s.w.t.) placed Ādam (a.s.) in this garden and commanded him to take care of the garden. Then Allāh (s.w.t) warned him saying: "Go ahead. It is ḥalāl for you to eat fruit from any of these trees, except for one. Do not eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, because you will die on the same day that you eat from it."c
The First Prophet
One of the great ṣaḥāba, Abū Dhar (r.a.), asked the prophet (s.a.w.): "Among the prophets, who was the first?" Rasul Allāh (s.a.w.) said: "Ādam (a.s.) is the first sent prophet. Allāh made him by His hand and put spirit in him and created him as a handsome human being." Another narration from Abū Dhar (r.a.) says: "I asked the messenger (s.a.w.): "Oh messenger of Allāh, was Ādam one of the prophets?" The messenger (s.a.w.) answered saying: "Of course, Ādam was the first prophet who spoke with Allāh directly."d
Naming the Animals
As Ādam (a.s.) walked through jannat ʿadn, \it he observed the beautiful perfection that surrounded him. Allāh (s.w.t.) brought all the creatures He had created to Ādam (a.s.)'s presence so that he (a.s.) could name them.e When he finished naming them, he realized that he lacked only one thing. There was no suitable companion for him.f
The Creation of Umm Ḥawwāʾ
18 Before Allāh the Highest presented the living creatures to Nabī Ādam, he revealed: "It is not good for Ādam to be alone. I will create a suitable companion for him."g So after Ādam (a.s.) had named the animals, Allāh created Umm Ḥawwāʾ from his side.h When Ādam (a.s.) first saw Umm Ḥawwāʾ, he rejoiced and said, "These bones are from my bones; this flesh is from my flesh; I name her 'woman', for she was created from man's rib."
\it So Umm Ḥawwāʾ's bone and flesh were originally taken from Ādam. \it As it is said in the Qurʾān al-Karīm: "People, be mindful of your Lord, who created you from a single soul, and from it created its mate..." (Al-Nisāʾ 4:1). That is why when a man gets married he leaves his parents, becomes joined to his wife, and the two of them become one body.
The Creation of the World
Allāh Almighty (s.w.t.) created the whole world in six days. In the surah Qāf there is a verse which says: "We created the heavens, the earth, and everything between, in six days," (Qāf 50:38 ). In the first two days Allāh separated the light from the darkness and the earth from heavens. During the other four days he gave beauty to the earth: he created mountains, the plant world and animal world for Banī* Ādami. And at the end, he created the most beautiful creation among the created — our first granddad and grandmom. It is written in Qurʾān: "We create man in the finest state." \it (Al-Tīn 95:4). In another āya Qurʾān says: "We have honoured the children of Ādam... and favoured them specially above many of those We have created." \it (Al-Isrāʾ 17:70). j
The World Without Sorrow
Gen1:31 Everything Allāh had created was good. "He gave everything its perfect form." (Al-Sajda 32:7). Gen1:11 When He commanded "Let there be!", all kinds of plants and trees which give fruit came out of the earth. Gen1:20 When He commanded "Let there be!", fish and different kinds of sea animals appeared in the water. Gen1:24 When He commanded "Let there bek!" all kinds of animals and different coloured birds came into existence. \it Allāh's blessing was seen in every thing.
Gen1:25 \it There were no diseases, no death, no sadness. The garden was full of trees that gave all kinds of fruits, and Ādam (a.s.) and Ḥawwāʾ were living happily in the presence of Allāh. They did not know yet what evil was.

Hazrat Ādam is the Khalīfa of Allāh on the Earth
Gen1:28 When Allāh (s.w.t.) created our first father and mother, He blessed them and commanded them saying this: "Be fruitful. Multiply and fill the earth. I have given this earth to you. Govern the fish, birds, and all animals living on the earth. Gen1:29 Here, I have given you for food all kinds of grain and trees which have seeds in their fruits."
\it So, it turns out that Hazrat Ādam was given the title of Khalīfa — the right to rule over the entire Earth. As it is written in the Qur'an, Allāh informed angels about this before He created Ādam (a.s.): "I am putting a Khalīfa on the earth." \it (Al-Baqara 2:30).
