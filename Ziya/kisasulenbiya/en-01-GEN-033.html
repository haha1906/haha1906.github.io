The brothers meet
Then Yaʿqūb (a.s.) looked and saw his brother ʿIsāw coming with 400 men. So he put his children with their own mothers, and put Bilhā and Zilfā in front with their children. Then he put Liʾā and her children in the middle, and Rāḥīl and Yūsuf at the end of the line. And he himself walked in front of everybody and before he reached his brother, he bowed down to the ground seven times. ʿIsāw came out toward his brother running and hugged him and kissed him. Both wept. Then ʿIsāw looked at the women and children and asked: "Who are these?" Yaʿqūb (a.s.) answered: "These are the children Allāh has given to your slave." Then Bilhā and Zilfā came with their children and bowed down before ʿIsāw. Then Liʾā came with her children, and finally Rāḥīl and Yūsuf came and bowed down. Then ʿIsāw asked: "What were the flocks I saw on the way?" Hazrat Yaʿqūb said: "I sent them as a gift so you would be kind to me." ʿIsāw said: "Oh my younger brother, keep your wealth for yourself. I have plenty of flocks." Yaʿqūb said, "No, brother, if I have indeed found your favour, please, accept my unworthy gift. You have accepted me graciously, now please accept my gifts also. I praise Allāh a thousand times that now I have plenty of wealth." Because Yaʿqūb (a.s.) kept insisting, ʿIsāw finally accepted the gifts. "Alright then, let us go. We'll go together", said ʿIsāw. But Hazrat Yaʿqūb said: "You see, brother, the children are still young. And we also have new born lambs and goats. If we make them walk harshly even for one day, they could all die. You go in front. I will go slowly behind with the flocks with my children. We'll see each other in al-Sharāhah." ʿIsāw said: "Alright. Then let me leave some of my men with you." But Yaʿqūb (a.s.) refused, saying "No need, do not trouble yourself." Then ʿIsāw returned to al-Sharāhah that same day with his men. Yaʿqūb (a.s.), however, went to Sūkūt with his family, and from there he went to Nāblis in the land of Kanʿān safe and sound.
So you see, Hazrat Yaʿqūb has returned to Kanʿān safe and sound. Do you remember, when he was running away from Kanʿān and from his brother, he had said: "If Allāh brings me back to my father's house in peace, I will worship Him only."
Thanksgiving of Yaʿqūb (a.s.) to Allāh
After arriving in Nāblis, Hazrat Yaʿqūb bought some land from the sons of the governor of Nāblis for 100 silver coins. There he built a camp outside the city. Then he said: "You are the god who gave me protection and kept me safe and sound," and slaughtered sacrifices to Allāh.
